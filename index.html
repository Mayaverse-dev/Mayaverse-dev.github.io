02

THE POWER OF STORIES

When a 9-year-old watched Star Trek, that story rewired her brain's reward system toward space exploration. Thirty years later, NASA engineer Swati Mohan guided Perseverance to Mars. Another story - about weapons of mass destruction and absolute evil - rewired an entire nation, launching a trillion-dollar war.

03

BIOLOGICAL IMPERATIVE

This isn't just anthropology. This is biology. We evolved to exchange stories as survival software - transmitting crucial warnings, methods, hacks. A cave painting warning which berries are poisonous, a folktale teaching wit and ingenuity, a film about standing up to injustice - these are algorithms passed down so each generation doesn't have to learn through fatal trial and error.

04

NARRATIVE MANIPULATION EXPERIMENT

In 1989, a biologist demonstrated this power of narrative. Male barn swallows with darker red chests attract more females - it's an evolutionary signal of genetic fitness. When he painted a rejected male's chest with a one-dollar marker, females found him irresistible. Even more irresistible than naturally red-chested males.

If a one-dollar marker can hack an entire species, imagine what a trillion-dollar narrative machine can do.
05

THE ENTERTAINMENT ENGINE

This same principle powers the $3 trillion entertainment industry.

Just as makeup amplifies signals of health and youth, our films, games, and stories amplify patterns our minds evolved to seek. Like our craving for sugar and fat in times of scarcity, our minds reward us to consume stories that once triggered survival-critical learning.

Tales of rivalry and romance, threat and conquest, good and evil, us and them. We call this hardwired reward mechanism "entertainment".

06

BEYOND ENTERTAINMENT

But stories do more than entertain, seduce and prevent mistakes. We evolved to use stories as a simulation engine, testing out futures before we live them. Through stories, we dream up better futures and reverse-engineer the steps to reach them.

They also simulate our worst nightmares, helping us recognize our self-harming behaviors and course correct.

Inferences drawn from these countless stories – of what to seek and what to avoid – become our values.

07

EMERGENCE OF LORE

Lore emerges - when thousands of stories interact. It is a living, breathing network of stories. Unlike a single narrative, lore allows for mutation, contradiction, and synthesis.

It's civilization's crystal ball, where millions of narrators and narratives collectively process our past and negotiate our futures.

As lore synthesizes thousands of stories, it becomes our shared mental model of reality - shaping our beliefs and, ultimately, our behaviors.

08

UNTAPPED POTENTIAL

Five narrative universes - Pokemon, Star Wars, Marvel, Harry Potter, and Lord of the Rings - have generated $280 billion by resonating with these same evolutionary instincts for survival, growth, and transcendence.

They combine the familiar with just enough of the impossible to grip our attention - hardwired to look out for changes within the familiar.

Yet they barely scratch the surface of lore’s true power.

09

THE CRISIS

We're using a quantum computer to run simple arithmetic. We have god-like technology but stone-age stories.

Today's challenges - climate crisis, AI revolution, economic disparity - are too complex for stories created for the industrial age or earlier. The 'good versus evil' stories that helped us survive in caves have become destructive in our polarized world.

We're running civilization-scale experiments without a simulator. We need a new crystal ball that shows us where we are going and where we could.

10

NEW MYTHOLOGY

We need new mythology. Immersive experiences as seductive as Marvel, but profoundly meaningful to us. As thrilling as PUBG, but more useful. As vast as the Mahabharata, but addressing humanity's most urgent challenges.

Lore that can help eight billion minds process unprecedented complexity and imagine better futures. To change reality, we must first change the stories we tell about it.

That's what we're building at Department of Lore - a narrative engine that will do for human meaning what AI does for data: transform collective experience into civilizational wisdom at planetary scale.

Enter Maya

We begin with Maya - a story universe that demonstrates the full potential of our vision. Set on the planet Samsaar, Maya introduces a civilization where a superintelligent network, grown from ancient trees, governs society through shared dreams. From birth, every child is tethered to this network, immersing in simulations far more vivid than reality itself.

This is a world where:
  • Multiple sapient species share an uneasy coexistence - from shape-shifting rakshasi to the enslaved yakshi
  • Immortal beings called the Divya use big data to steer civilization's future
  • Ancient biology meshes with advanced technology, creating a dream network that remembers every hope, fear, and desire
  • Control masquerades as benevolence, and progress shadows inequality

The First Story

The Maya Universe will launch in March 2025, beginning with the novel "Seed Takes Root."

The story ignites when a young man discovers he's "nirmaya" - invisible to the all-seeing Maya network. As the Divya announce their trials offering godhood and immortality, he faces a choice: compete for ultimate power or challenge the very foundation of their world.
